Talking to Learn.

"I needed a way to talk about what I just learned."

Like many developers, I found the traditional "tutorial loop" broken. Watching videos creates a false sense of competence; staring at a blank editor creates task paralysis. Often, the only way to break the deadlock and compile those abstract concepts was to talk to myself—or rubber-duck to thin air.

One thing led to another, and I built a pipeline. ZenNode was created to ingest chaotic, rambling audio dumps and force them into strict, actionable mental maps. It’s the computational execution of the Feynman Technique.

Built to be Hacked (OSS).

ZenNode isn't a walled garden. It's an open-source, strongly-typed Python pipeline meant to be modified. Built on LangGraph, the architecture is entirely component-based. Swap Groq for your local Whisper instance, re-route nodes to an on-premise LLaMa, or change the checkpointer from SQLite to Postgres.

Why ZenNode was built

"I wanted to set up a place where I can dump screenshots, texts, and direct voice notes, rather than annotating everything perfectly."

When learning SOTA tools—like LangGraph, MCP, or complex GenAI architectures—the biggest hurdle isn't finding information. It's the paralysis of having a gazillion things to do, leading to an irrational fear of touching the codebase.

ZenNode was built to solve three core problems:

  • Building coding confidence: Eliminating the fear of complex codebases by breaking them down visually and textually.
  • Retaining knowledge: Processing scattered video tutorials, AI chats, and raw notes into cohesive mental maps.
  • Executing momentum: Pushing through task paralysis by minimizing setup friction.

The initial thought was just an infinite document to endlessly paste learnings. But passive dumping doesn't equal learning. The real cognitive unlock happens when you teach someone else—an active execution of the Feynman Technique.

Instead of starting a YouTube channel just to rubber-duck my architectural thoughts, ZenNode was built to be that listener. It takes minimal effort to dump your context and run the pipeline, returning a factually accurate markdown vault (checked against the latest docs) that helps form a rock-solid mental map.
